## Introduction to Generative Design

Generative design is a design exploration process where designers or engineers input design goals and constraints into software, which then generates numerous design alternatives that meet those requirements. The process leverages cloud computing and AI to explore all possible permutations of a solution, quickly generating design alternatives.

### Benefits

- **Innovation**: Discovers novel design solutions
- **Efficiency**: Reduces design time and material usage
- **Performance**: Optimizes for weight, strength, and other factors
- **Cost Reduction**: Identifies cost-effective designs
- **Sustainability**: Minimizes material waste

## Key Concepts and Terminology

### 1. Design Space
- **Definition**: The volume where the design can exist
- **Fixed Geometry**: Areas that cannot be modified
- **Preserved Regions**: Areas that must remain unchanged

### 2. Load Cases
- **Static Loads**: Constant forces
- **Dynamic Loads**: Time-varying forces
- **Thermal Loads**: Temperature variations
- **Fatigue**: Cyclic loading conditions

### 3. Constraints
- **Geometric**: Size, shape, and connection points
- **Manufacturing**: Production method limitations
- **Material**: Available material properties
- **Performance**: Required strength, stiffness, etc.

## Best Practices

### 1. Start with Clear Objectives
- Define success criteria

### 2. Balance Performance and Manufacturability
