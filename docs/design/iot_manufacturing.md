## Introduction to IoT in Manufacturing

Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) refers to the network of physical devices, vehicles, and other items embedded with electronics, software, sensors, and network connectivity that enable these objects to collect and exchange data.

### Benefits of IoT in Manufacturing

- **Predictive Maintenance**: Reduce downtime by predicting equipment failures
- **Asset Tracking**: Real-time monitoring of materials and products
- **Quality Control**: Automated inspection and quality assurance
- **Energy Management**: Optimize energy consumption
- **Supply Chain Optimization**: Improved inventory and logistics management
- **Worker Safety**: Monitor environmental conditions and worker health

## Key Components of IIoT

### 1. Smart Sensors
- Temperature, pressure, vibration, and other environmental sensors
- RFID and NFC tags for tracking
- Vision systems for quality inspection

### 2. Connectivity
- Wired and wireless communication protocols
- Industrial networks (PROFINET, EtherCAT, etc.)
- 5G and LPWAN for wireless connectivity

### 3. Edge Devices
- Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs)
- Industrial PCs (IPCs)
- Edge gateways

### 4. Cloud Platforms
- Data storage and processing
- Analytics and machine learning
- Remote monitoring and control

### 5. Applications
- Manufacturing Execution Systems (MES)
- Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) integration
- Custom dashboards and visualization tools

## Sensors and Devices

### Common Sensor Types
- **Vibration Sensors**: Detect equipment wear and tear
- **Temperature Sensors**: Monitor thermal conditions
- **Pressure Sensors**: Measure fluid and gas pressures
- **Proximity Sensors**: Detect object presence
- **Flow Sensors**: Measure liquid and gas flow rates
- **Gas Sensors**: Detect hazardous gases
- **Humidity Sensors**: Monitor environmental conditions
